The pitch curator plays an incredibly influential role by meticulously preparing the central twenty-two-yard playing strip. By adjusting grass length and soil moisture levels, these experts can alter how the ball bounces and behaves during a match. A hard, dry surface with minimal grass typically favors fast bowlers who want to generate extra bounce and carry. Conversely, as a pitch dries out over multiple days, it begins to crack and crumble, heavily favoring tactical spin bowlers. Ground staff must monitor local weather forecasts closely to protect the clay foundation from sudden, damaging downpours.
